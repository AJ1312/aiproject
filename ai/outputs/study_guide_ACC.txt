### **üìù 9. VIT Exam Strategy for ACC (FAT Specific)**

*   **Read Problems Carefully (Multiple Times):** In competitive coding, a small detail in the problem statement or constraints can change the entire approach. Don't rush.
*   **Analyze Constraints:** `N` up to 10^5 suggests O(N log N) or O(N), `N` up to 1000 suggests O(N^2), etc. This immediately guides your choice of algorithm.
*   **Think Before Coding:**
    1.  **Understand the Problem:** Paraphrase it in your own words.
    2.  **Identify Data Structures/Algorithms:** Which concepts from ACC are relevant here?
    3.  **Develop an Approach:** Outline the steps. Consider edge cases.
    4.  **Analyze Complexity:** What's the time and space complexity of your proposed solution? Is it optimal given the constraints?
    5.  **Write Pseudo-code/Logic:** Before jumping to code, lay out your logic. This helps catch errors early.
*   **Modular Coding:** Break down complex solutions into smaller, manageable functions.
*   **Test Thoroughly:** Always test your code with provided sample cases, and critically, come up with your own edge cases (empty input, single element, max constraints, duplicate values, negative numbers if applicable).
*   **Time Management in Exam:** Allocate time per question based on its weight and your perceived difficulty. Don't get stuck on one problem for too long. If you're stuck, move on and revisit if time permits.
*   **Focus on Correctness and Optimization:** For ACC, simply getting *an* answer isn't enough; it needs to be the *correct* answer within the given time and memory limits.
